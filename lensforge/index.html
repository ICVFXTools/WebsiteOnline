Capture guidelines for reliable calibration

  • Use 15–30 images — more is better, diminishing returns above 40
  • Cover all four corners and the centre of the frame across your set
  • Tilt the board ±30° in X, Y, and Z axes between shots
  • Keep the board flat — mount on rigid aluminium or foam board
  • Avoid motion blur, focus the board sharply, use consistent exposure
  • Shoot at your working aperture (not wide-open, not diffraction-limited)

Lower is better. <0.5 px = excellent  |  <1.0 px = acceptable  |  >1.0 px = investigate capture quality

Parameter Reference

fl_mm
Estimated real focal length in mm — computed from pixel focal length × sensor size ÷ image width/height.
fx / fy
Pixel focal lengths. May differ slightly for non-square pixels or anamorphic lenses.
c1 (cx) / c2 (cy)
Principal point in pixels. Ideally close to image centre. Large offsets may indicate decentring.
k1 / k2
Radial distortion. Negative k1 = barrel (typical for wide primes). Positive k1 = pincushion.
k3 / p3
Third radial distortion coefficient. OpenCV calls this k3; some VFX pipelines label it p3. Only non-zero in Extended and Rational models.
p1 / p2
Tangential (decentering) distortion. Usually very small (<0.01). Large values suggest a misaligned lens element.
RMS
Root Mean Square reprojection error in pixels — how accurately the solved model predicts the detected corner positions.
